Data Sources & Methodology

Transparency is at the core of EatClever. Here we outline the sources, methods, and limitations behind the nutrition data presented on this platform.

Our Sources

EatClever draws nutrition data from the following authoritative, peer-reviewed sources:

  • USDA FoodData Central
  • WHO Nutrition
  • NIH Office of Dietary Supplements
  • Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health
  • European Food Safety Authority

Our Approach

We cross-reference multiple sources to ensure accuracy. Nutrient values represent standard serving sizes and are updated regularly. Our comparisons and recommendations follow established dietary guidelines while remaining practical for everyday use.

Limitations

Nutrition science is complex and evolving. Individual needs vary based on age, health conditions, activity level, and other factors. EatClever provides general guidance and should not replace professional medical or dietary advice. Nutrient values can vary based on growing conditions, preparation methods, and product brands.
